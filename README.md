PracticeOS

AI-powered admin and workflow system for Australian allied health clinicians.

Built on Google Workspace + Claude AI — no new software, no steep learning curve. PracticeOS gives physios, OTs, speech pathologists, and other allied health professionals a simple daily system to manage clients, reports, and follow-ups.


What It Does

PracticeOS replaces scattered notes, missed follow-ups, and hours of report writing with three daily habits:


Morning scan — a 60-second client brief before each appointment
Voice log — hands-free post-session dictation that auto-generates a summary and action list
End-of-day debrief — a shared team tracker and AI-drafted reports and GP emails


Key Features


🎧 Audio brief — client history read aloud via AirPods or smart glasses between sessions
🎙️ Voice dictation — post-session notes logged in under 3 minutes, hands-free
🤖 AI assistant — Claude AI drafts reports, GP referral emails, and daily action summaries from bullet-point notes
📋 Client hub — one Google Drive folder per client: profile, session notes, reports, and correspondence
✅ Follow-up tracker — shared Google Sheet showing every outstanding action across the whole team
📱 Mobile + desktop — works on any device, no app install required

The Bigger Picture — PracticeOS as a Product

This system was piloted at Apex Physio in NSW, Australia. The workflow is now being productised as PracticeOS — a subscription offering for allied health clinicians across Australia.

Target market: 40,000+ registered allied health professionals in Australia across physiotherapy, OT, speech pathology, chiropractic, and podiatry.

Pricing model: AUD $49/month (solo clinician) · AUD $39/clinician/month (2–8 clinicians) · Custom (9+)

Key differentiator: PracticeOS is not a practice management system. It fills the gap that Cliniko, Nookal, and Power Diary leave — the human side of admin: follow-ups, AI-assisted writing, voice dictation, and daily workflow habits. It sits alongside whatever system a clinic already uses.

Compliance Notes (Australia)


Patient data stored in Google Workspace must use Australian data residency settings (required under the Privacy Act 1988 and Australian Privacy Principles)
Video recording of clients requires explicit informed consent under the APPs
Smart glasses and wearable devices in clinical settings require a consent framework — legal review in progress
All AI-drafted correspondence is reviewed by the clinician before sending
